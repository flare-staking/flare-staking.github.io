How it works

Flare Staking in three steps

Stake from your wallet, see the live staking terms before signing, and keep the entire flow anchored to on-chain confirmation.

Step 01

Connect your wallet

Open the app on Flare and connect through your wallet or WalletConnect. You keep the private keys and approve every transaction yourself.

Step 02

Choose FLR and validator

Select the FLR amount, review validator options, and check the current reward, lock, and transaction details shown inside the app.

Step 03

Confirm and monitor

Sign the staking transaction from your wallet, then track pending and confirmed activity through the app or Flarescan.

Why stake

Turn idle FLR into network participation

Stake without custody transfer

Flare Staking is built for self-custody. The app prepares the transaction, your wallet displays it, and nothing moves unless you approve it.

Know the time window

Staking, reward updates, and unstaking availability depend on live network state. The app shows the current status before and after you sign.

Plan around costs

Each stake, claim, or unstake action uses the network gas shown by your wallet. Review cost, validator, and timing details before confirming.

Flare Staking focuses on FLR on the Flare mainnet, an EVM-compatible layer one built around data protocols.

FAQ

Flare Staking questions

01

What is Flare Staking?

Flare Staking is a non-custodial way to stake FLR, support validator-based network security, and follow rewards from your own wallet.

02

Which asset do I stake?

The staking flow is built around FLR, the native asset of Flare. The app shows supported assets and any required network context before you sign.

03

How long does staking take?

The transaction must be confirmed on-chain, and any stake activation or withdrawal timing follows live Flare network rules shown in the app.

04

What does it cost?

You pay the network gas required for the action you sign. The wallet and app show cost details before the transaction is submitted.

05

Is Flare Staking safe?

It is non-custodial, but staking still carries smart contract, validator, wallet, and market risks. Verify the domain and read each wallet prompt carefully.

06

Which chains are supported?

Flare Staking centers on Flare mainnet support. See the current network list on the supported chains page.
